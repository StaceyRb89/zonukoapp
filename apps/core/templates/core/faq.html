Parent FAQ

Clear answers to the questions UK parents ask most — so you can decide with confidence.

Quick answers for busy parents

Safety, supervision, screen use, and suitability — all in one place.

Is Zonuko safe for children?

Yes. Zonuko is designed to remove the risks common in social and learning platforms.

There is no direct messaging, no public comments or follower counts, and no open profiles.

All sharing is optional, parent-approved, and moderated before being showcased. Children use avatars and usernames only, and activity happens in age-appropriate spaces.

How much screen time does this involve?

Screens are used intentionally — for planning, creating, and reflecting — not passive watching or scrolling.

Some projects involve design or programming, but screen time is always purposeful and linked to creating something.

Will my child need constant supervision?

Projects are designed for independent work, with light supervision as appropriate. Parents are encouraged to assess when input is needed and step back wherever possible.

Some projects involve materials or tools that may require light supervision, and this is always clearly indicated.

What if my child loses interest or skips a week?

That’s okay. Zonuko isn’t built around streaks, pressure, or punishments.

Projects are flexible and designed to be picked up again later and fit around busy weeks.

Progress is about confidence and curiosity, not completion.

Is this suitable if my child isn’t “technical”?

Yes. Zonuko isn’t about being good at science or coding.

Projects include creative design, problem-solving, storytelling, and real-world thinking.

Every learner has an entry point — building, sketching, testing, improving, or reflecting.

How do the age tracks work?

Zonuko uses three flexible age tracks:

  • Imaginauts (6–10): guided, playful building
  • Navigators (11–13): deeper challenges and experimentation
  • Trailblazers (14–16): open-ended design and real-world problem solving

Children aren’t locked in — parents can adjust tracks based on confidence and interest.

What do parents actually see?

Parents can see which projects were attempted, reflections saved by their child, and a private skills portfolio over time.

Nothing is shared publicly without approval.

Is Zonuko tutoring or exam prep?

No. Zonuko is a calm, project-based learning platform designed to build confidence, resilience, and creative thinking — not to replace school or teach to tests.

Why should I join as a founding family?

Founding families get early access before public launch, help shape what Zonuko becomes, and can lock in a lower founding price while their membership remains active.

There’s no obligation — just a chance to be part of building something thoughtful from the start.

What happens after I sign up?

You’ll receive a launch email when Zonuko opens, clear instructions to get started, and the option to give feedback as a founding family.

No spam. No pressure.
