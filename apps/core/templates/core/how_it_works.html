How Zonuko works (for parents)

A calm weekly rhythm of hands-on projects — with clear guidance on when adult support is recommended, and parent-controlled sharing.

Screen use is purposeful (planning, creating, reflecting) — not passive watching.

Three simple steps

Zonuko is designed to be easy to start and easy to stick with — even on busy weeks.

1. Pick a project

Choose from a small set of weekly projects matched to your child’s age track (Imaginauts 6–10, Navigators 11–13, Trailblazers 14–16).

2. Build offline (with clear guidance)

Kids spend most of their time making with everyday materials. Each project includes simple setup notes and clearly flags when adult support or supervision is recommended.

3. Capture progress (optional)

Your child can save a photo and a short reflection to their private portfolio. Sharing is always optional and only happens with parent approval.

A week at a glance

A simple rhythm that keeps momentum without turning into another commitment.

  • Monday: New weekly schedule drops (quick wins + one deeper build)
  • Any day: 30–60 minutes of hands-on making, at your pace
  • Optional: Save a reflection to the private portfolio
  • Weekend idea: Family team-up projects (optional)
  • Sunday: Low-pressure "Showcase" moments (parent-approved only)

Built for creativity without the pressures of social media

Quick guardrails you can scan in seconds.

  • No direct messaging
  • Parent-controlled accounts
  • Admin moderation before showcasing
  • Separate age-appropriate community spaces

More safeguards include no public comments or follower counts, avatars/usernames only, and admin review before featuring work.

Parent dashboard: what you'll see

See progress clearly without hovering.

  • Projects attempted and child reflections
  • Skills portfolio history over time
  • Optional printable portfolios for offline records
  • Full visibility and control over sharing

Project history

What they tried, what they changed, and what they learned.

Skills over time

A private portfolio that shows growth across weeks and projects.

Sharing controls

Approve what's saved or shared — or keep everything private.

Guidance at a glance

Notes on materials, setup, and when adult support is recommended.

What Zonuko is not

  • Not tutoring or exam prep
  • Not open social networking
  • Not a gamified app built around streaks or rewards

Zonuko is a calm, structured space where kids learn by building, confidence grows through real progress.
